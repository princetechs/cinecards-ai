# aiscreens Term Preview Visual Identity

## Style Prompt

Short educational motion cards for aiscreens: practical, cinematic, warm, and clear. The visuals should feel like a clean cinematography notebook turned into motion: simple frame guides, bold labels, clear camera language, and small cinematic accents. Every preview teaches one idea in under eight seconds.

## Colors

- Ink: `#171717` for primary text and deep panels.
- Bone: `#F8F5EF` for the canvas.
- Sand: `#E6DDD1` for borders and muted surfaces.
- Clay: `#E4572E` for teaching emphasis and active frame lines.
- Quiet: `#7A7268` for secondary text.

## Typography

- Display: `Space Grotesk`, fallback `Arial`, sans-serif.
- Body: `Inter`, fallback `Arial`, sans-serif.

## Motion Rules

- Use calm, purposeful motion: frame reveals, soft slides, camera-like pushes, and timeline wipes.
- Keep all videos between 5 and 8 seconds.
- One concept per video. No decorative motion that does not teach the term.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not use generic stock-video motion.
- Do not rely on a still image with only a zoom effect.
- Do not add dense paragraphs or tiny text.
- Do not use real human likenesses.
- Do not exceed the card preview's teaching purpose.
